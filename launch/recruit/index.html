WHO TO RECRUIT

PLAYTESTERS

Fresh players

People who can try a public browser build and tell us where they got confused, delighted, or blocked.

FLIGHT TESTERS

Control-feel people

Players who like arcade flight, dogfighting, shmups, racing, or vehicle tuning. Ask them specifically about Sortie and any future flight slice.

REVIEWERS

Careful triagers

People who write clear reproduction steps, stay kind, stay scoped, and can recommend approve / needs-work / defer.

WHERE TO POST FIRST

  1. Warm circle: friends, existing supporters, trusted players, and people who already understand the project tone.
  2. Owned channels: itch devlog, Ko-fi update, GitHub README/support hub, personal site, newsletter, or pinned social post.
  3. Small communities: indie game, arcade flight, shmup, cozy game, accessibility, or pixel-art groups where playtest requests are allowed.
  4. After signal appears: ask the best playtesters if they want to apply as reviewers.

SCREENING RULES FOR PA

SignalAccept / escalateReject / no access
Scope One narrow lane, such as controls or link checks. Wants to review everything, run the project, or publish builds.
Sample review Specific, reproducible, kind, and short. Vague praise, insults, demands, or no evidence.
Access request Understands public comments first. Asks for secrets, private builds, billing, merge rights, or player data.
Privacy Uses public-only GitHub handle and general device info. Posts private contact details or asks players for identity details.

Do not recruit by promising payment, official staff status, repository access, or public release control. The promise is simple: help shape public previews, get heard, and maybe earn a narrow reviewer role if the pilot is useful.

ONE-STEP HANDOFF

If Daniel or the PA only has time for one action today: copy the short post, send it to 5-10 trusted people or one small community, then check the application and feedback queues the next day.
